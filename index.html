Intro

Born and raised in Springfield, MO! This May I graduate with a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science: Software Engineering, with a minor in Cyber Security.

At the beginning of 2020 I was contemplating escaping from retail sales, which I had been working at since the end of 2017. Then along came Covid, and with the time I had during the shutdown, I did some self-searching and decided I needed a change. Always a fan of games, computers, and technology in general, I decided to take a look at Computer science. I've always been a more technical and logic-based thinker, excelling in reading, math, and sciences. After researching the subject and seeing what was in store if I chose this path, I eagerly applied for the program at Drury university.

I chose computer science for a few reasons. First, I enjoy the rules and logic structure. As an avid fan of Magic: The Gathering and Dungeons & Dragons, I love working with rule-based systems, so the natural logic of the science and rules regarding different programming languages is both enjoyable and comes naturally. Second, I enjoy the satisfaction of the solution. It is a great feeling when implementing a solution or fixing a problem in the code. It feels like solving a puzzle, and the "ah hah!" moment that regularly occurs is something I will never get tired of. Finally, I enjoy creating solutions and solving problems. It's a little hard to describe, but like the "ah hah!" feeling, the satisfaction of solving a problem or creating something for someone is fantastic. The act of creation itself is wonderful, even more so when that creation fulfils its purpose meaningfully.

eMission

eMission is a mobile application that allows users to track their personal carbon emissions. Built with Flutter, a NodeJS backend, and a MySQL database.

https://github.com/Developer-DUCS/eMission

eMission is the final works created by myself and some of my closest classmates to conclude our time at Drury. This initially started in my Junior year Software Engineering class where we covered the core principles of Software Engineering, such as the different development cycles (Waterfall, Agile, DevOps, etc.), requirements models & analysis models for software systems, architecture diagrams, testing, UML diagrams, use cases, and user stories. Tasked with coming up with an idea for a software project revolving around the United Nation's 17 Sustainable Development Goals, we tossed around ideas until I pitched a mobile application for user to track their personal vehicle carbon emissions, named eMission. We decided to continue this hypothetical project into the capstone course, R&D and teamed up to make this project a reality.
